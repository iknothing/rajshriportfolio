About Me

Rajshri dons multiple hats, from being a social worker to an actress. She has a brilliant body of work, and the new year has begun on a striking note with the global success of 'Trial By Fire' on Netflix. The show has garnered praise from critics as well as viewers, and it is also trending globally on IMDB with an 8.3 rating from viewers. Speaking about her body of work, Rajshri was a part of Sacred Games Season 1 and captured our attention like no other. The series became a hit globally. In the suspense drama Fame Game, Rajshri was applauded for her performance and also shared the screen space with an ensemble cast. Her projects 'Angry Indian Goddesses' and 'S Durga' were both brilliant, and the latter won the 'Hivos Tiger Award' at the Rotterdam Film Festival. Vijay Jayapal's 'Nirvana-Inn' was premiered at BIFF, and Rajshri was praised for her acting skills. She has become one of the most bankable actresses of our time, with such a promising body of work. Her interest in social work started after the Nepal earthquake. Rajshri worked with an international NGO, and in 2015, she executed a rainwater harvesting project that changed the lives of the villagers for good. Encouraged by the success, in 2018, she created the Nabhangan Foundation (www.nabhanganfoundation.org) to broaden her efforts towards sustainable village development. During the pandemic, her team worked with more than 30 villages and is strongly working towards making them self-sustainable. She recently finished building two eco-sensitive schools in different villages and is in the process of making education accessible to more and more people.
